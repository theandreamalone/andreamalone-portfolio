## Ranked skills (breadth + depth)

Ordered by depth and repetition across projects, not alphabetically.

1. **AI experience and product design** — AI dashboards, conversational experiences, context-aware assistance, automation, recommendations, and decision-support tools.
2. **Complex enterprise workflow design** — simplifying network operations, incident management, governance, marketing analytics, compliance, and multi-step operational processes.
3. **AI interaction design** — chat, voice, shortcuts, follow-up prompts, retries, interruptions, expanded views, approvals, and human handoffs.
4. **AI response and output architecture** — intents, structured response blocks, evidence, supporting and conflicting signals, missing information, recommendations, risks, timelines, and next actions.
5. **AI problem framing and use-case strategy** — identifying where AI should summarize, analyze, recommend, automate, escalate, or remain out of the workflow.
6. **AI trust, transparency, and explainability** — signal summaries, source evidence, data-quality indicators, uncertainty, review requirements, and user control.
7. **End-to-end product design** — discovery through flows, wireframes, high-fidelity design, prototyping, validation, handoff, and iteration.
8. **Information architecture and workflow mapping** — organizing complex systems, roles, content, dependencies, states, and navigation.
9. **Dashboard and data-visualization design** — executive summaries, operational monitoring, Power BI analytics, incident briefings, trends, severity, and status visualization.
10. **AI governance and responsible AI design** — oversight, compliance, risk management, auditability, privacy, accessibility, and ethical AI considerations.
11. **Context and input design for AI** — determining user inputs, system context, workflow state, data sources, permissions, and information required for useful responses.
12. **Human-AI collaboration design** — balancing AI assistance with review, correction, escalation, accountability, and human decision authority.
13. **Responsive product design** — adapting complex AI and enterprise workflows across desktop, tablet, and mobile.
14. **Design systems and scalable interaction patterns** — reusable components, states, visual language, accessibility patterns, and cross-product consistency.
15. **Cross-functional product leadership** — aligning product, engineering, data, business, client, and design stakeholders around feasible solutions.
16. **Rapid prototyping and iterative design** — exploring multiple concepts, testing interaction patterns, incorporating feedback, and preparing release-ready designs.
17. **User research and behavioral analysis** — interviews, testing, surveys, heatmaps, attention maps, session recordings, and research synthesis.
18. **Operational prioritization and alert design** — severity, urgency, unread states, risk flags, notifications, incident ranking, and escalation cues.
19. **Accessibility and inclusive design** — WCAG compliance, reading order, landmarks, touch targets, responsive accessibility, and equitable AI interactions.
20. **Technical design and engineering collaboration** — feasibility assessment, detailed handoff, front-end implementation knowledge, APIs, React, and design-to-code workflows.
21. **Evaluation and continuous AI improvement** — feedback capture, response-quality assessment, usability validation, governance review, and iterative model-experience refinement.
22. **Legacy modernization and workflow optimization** — replacing fragmented or inefficient tools with cohesive, scalable, user-centered systems.
23. **Conversion, engagement, and product adoption** — improving interpretation, task completion, traffic, retention, client adoption, and operational efficiency.
24. **Personalization and adaptive experience strategy** — situation-aware guidance, audience-specific content, behavioral adaptation concepts, and persona-aware portfolio experiences.

## Cross-cutting principles

- **Engineering handoff is universal.** Applies to every enterprise project — do not treat as differentiating skill; treat as baseline.
- **Cross-functional leadership is universal at this career stage.** Every project involves leading design, clients, and development.
- **Depth signals over breadth signals.** When choosing skills for a case study, pick the 5–7 most demonstrable, not the full applicable list. Overtagging dilutes the signal.
