I evaluated the platform as a connected system rather than treating each product or screen in isolation. My cross-product lens centered on operational clarity, shared-pattern consistency, role- and context-based prioritization, trustworthy AI behavior, accessibility, and engineering feasibility. Across the independent sub-apps, I examined navigation logic, information hierarchy, component use, interaction states, responsive behavior, and the consistency of AI responses and escalation paths. This surfaced recurring issues such as conflicting interaction patterns, inconsistent state treatments, duplicated solutions, and places where users could be required to relearn similar behaviors across products.

I documented findings through ongoing design reviews, Figma comparisons, QA and handoff checks, workflow diagrams, and implementation-delta notes. I organized them by user impact, operational risk, recurrence across products, implementation effort, release timing, and alignment with established patterns. This created a shared basis for deciding which issues required immediate correction, which should be reconciled through shared components or design-system guidance, and which needed further product or engineering alignment.
