I would measure the dashboard by whether it improves incident
decision-making, not by whether users open it. The strongest signals are
repeat use during active incidents, faster situation understanding, fewer
duplicate status requests, and whether the experience supports escalation,
communication, or resource-allocation decisions.

The dashboard saw daily use during live incidents, with leaders returning
to it as new information emerged — meaningful adoption evidence under real
operational conditions, not first-week curiosity. Further quantitative
validation — time from incident detection to identifying the
highest-priority issue, reduction in manually assembled incident
summaries — would confirm the operational impact those usage patterns
suggest.
