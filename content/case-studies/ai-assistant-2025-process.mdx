**I proposed replacing the inherited welcome screen** — a static grid of context cards on standard portal chrome — with a purpose-built AI entry point. An assistant-selector dropdown exposes agent routing to the user instead of hiding it, which matters for trust and control once several specialized agents are in play. Suggested prompts surface high-value queries above the fold to cut blank-page friction on first use. What changed: the landing experience stopped looking like a portal with a chat bolted on, and started reading as an AI-native starting point.

From that entry point, I established the core conversational structure in the first version, then used the move from 1.0 to 2.0 to make reasoning and trust visible rather than expanding the interface through unrelated features: the later design added a visible Thinking state and per-response access to sources, supporting tables, and response time so users could verify how an answer was produced; it also introduced response-quality feedback, organization and business-unit scoping, and segment-aware follow-up prompts, shifting the experience from "the AI answered" to "the AI answered, and here is the evidence needed to assess it"; these decisions propagated into the Pro and Lite capability modes, independent display themes, responsive breakpoints, and specification boards so engineering received one coherent interaction system rather than disconnected variants.
