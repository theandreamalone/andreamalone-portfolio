The platform's navigation was already organized around user workflows rather than product boundaries, a direction established before I joined the program. My role was to support and extend that architecture through implementation, reducing navigation to two levels while reconciling entry points across Network Aware, Fault IQ, Communique, FCC Notifications, PSAP/E-911, Feedback Management, Weather Incident Brief, Incident Manager, the AI Dashboard, and AI Chat. I aligned these experiences within four workflow groupings—monitoring, incident response, communications and notifications, and administration—while preserving the separations required by product ownership and technical boundaries. I treated the information architecture as a reconciliation problem: closing the gap between the intended workflow model and how the underlying products had evolved, rather than forcing a full consolidation. This judgment helped the platform feel more connected at the experience level without implying that its independent products had become one system.
