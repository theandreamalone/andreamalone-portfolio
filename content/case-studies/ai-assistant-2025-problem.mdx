The previous experience was a conventional corporate portal organized around top-level navigation, marketing-style content categories, and search, which required users to browse through Products and Services, Resources, and Governance rather than ask for a direct answer; the client had already chosen conversational AI, so my design problem was not proving that AI belonged, but defining how an ask-and-answer product should replace the browse-and-navigate model while remaining scoped and governable. I centered the experience on one "Ask anything" entry point, bounded by two independent scoping controls: Focus Area narrows the question to a subject or business domain, while Search Base limits which approved documents, datasets, or knowledge sources the assistant searches. Together they keep the entry point from behaving like an unrestricted chatbot, giving the answer visible boundaries rather than an implied one. I addressed the remaining retrieval barriers through prompt libraries, filtered conversation history, and administrative usage visibility so the assistant could support open-ended questions without becoming an unstructured black box.
